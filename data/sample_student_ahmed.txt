Q1: Artificial Intelligence means making computers think like humans. It is the ability of machines to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. 

Examples:
- Google Maps uses AI to find the best route and predict traffic.
- Netflix uses AI to recommend movies based on what you have watched before.

---

Q2: Machine Learning is a type of AI where computers learn from data without being explicitly programmed. 

Supervised learning is when the computer learns from labeled data. For example, teaching a spam filter by showing it examples of spam and not spam emails.

Unsupervised learning is when the computer finds patterns in data without labels. For example, grouping customers by their buying habits.

---

Q3: A DBMS is software that helps store, manage and retrieve data in a structured way. Examples include MySQL and Oracle.

Advantages over flat-file systems:
1. Data is not repeated (no redundancy). In flat files the same data may be stored in many places.
2. Data is more secure. DBMS allows us to set permissions so only authorized users can access data.
3. Multiple users can access the database at the same time which is not possible in flat files.

Q4: IoT means connecting everyday objects to the internet so they can send and receive data.

Pakistan applications:
1. Smart agriculture - farmers can use IoT sensors to check soil moisture and water their crops automatically. This is very helpful in Punjab where farming is important.
2. Smart electricity meters - IoT meters can send electricity readings automatically to WAPDA without a person visiting each home.

---

Q5: Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks and data from hackers and cyber attacks.

Three common cyber threats:
1. Phishing - when hackers send fake emails pretending to be a bank or company to steal your password and personal information.
2. Malware - harmful software that damages your computer or steals data. Viruses and ransomware are examples.
3. Hacking - unauthorized access to your accounts or computer systems.

---

Q6: Cloud computing means using the internet to store and access data and programs instead of your own computer.

Public cloud is available to everyone over the internet. Example is Google Drive where anyone can store files.

Private cloud is only for one organization and is more secure. Example is a hospital using its own private servers for patient data.
